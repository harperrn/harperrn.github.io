Minimalistic Design Process

  • - Plan -

    Planning consists of talking to you, the consumer, about your wants and needs. An assessment will be made on the required elements and a wireframe or a designer's schematic will be built. Based off this, the overall layout of the site can be visualized and adjustments can be made before building takes place.

  • - Design -

    Based off the wireframe, images, text, and other crucial elements are put in place and can be viewed in a traditional web browser such as Chrome or Firefox. During this time, a color scheme is chosen from a company average palette and implimented into the webpage.

  • - Evaluate -

    While the initial building process is coming to a hault, a meeting is set up between the designer and the customer to discuss what elements need to be changed. A primary stage of revision is conducted and another customer assessment is organized.

  • - Revise -

    A primary stage of revision is conducted and another customer assessment is organized. With this, the semi-final version is shown. Another set of more minor revisions may happen succeeding this process until a satisfactory final draft is ready to be pushed to the hosting server.

Our minimalistic design process consists of four steps: plan, design, critique, and revise changes. This process allows for the most efficient workflow. This process can be completed by one designer and allows for the overall cost of building the site to be cut by at least 40%. Errors and revisions required to be adjusted are resolved quickly and efficinetly by one designer.
